Detailed Description

A data structure that represents a planar subdivision of a 2D surface.

Note
DCEL (Doubly Connected Edge List) is a data structure commonly used to represent planar subdivisions of a 2D surface.
It stores information about the vertices, edges, and faces of the subdivision, along with their relationships to one another. Specifically, each vertex stores a pointer to an incident edge, each edge stores pointers to its incident vertices, its twin edge (if any), and the face on its right side, and each face stores a pointer to an incident edge on its boundary.
DCEL is often used in computational geometry algorithms, such as computing Voronoi diagrams and Delaunay triangulations.
